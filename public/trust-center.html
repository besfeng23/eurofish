Credibility standard

No hype. No unsupported dominance claims.

Documents should support buyer decisions. Sensitive files should not be published publicly. Proof is shared responsibly with qualified buyers.

Verification posture

What buyers can expect.

These rules make the business look serious without pretending to have public proof that has not yet been uploaded.

Qualified document access

Relevant business, accreditation, quotation, and shipment-related documents may be shared with qualified buyers after request and verification.

Redaction by default

Document numbers, personal information, signatures, account details, or sensitive supplier information should be redacted before sharing publicly or broadly.

Principal accountability

Serious buyer inquiries are positioned for direct principal-level oversight, especially where timing, volume, and documentation matter.

Transaction-specific terms

Availability, volume, pricing, order requirements, shipment window, and receiving plan are confirmed per transaction, not implied as fixed public promises.

Receiving readiness

Milkfish fry and imported flowers require receiving details, destination, target date, and handoff coordination before serious quotation.

Buyer qualification

The site asks for company name, buyer type, product, quantity, urgency, target date, destination, and document needs to separate serious buyers from vague inquiries.

Before quote

Buyer details that improve credibility both ways.

  • Product needed: milkfish fry, imported flowers, or documents.
  • Estimated quantity or order range.
  • Target date and receiving location.
  • Buyer type: farm, nursery, distributor, florist, event supplier, or procurement officer.
  • Contact person and mobile/Viber number.
  • Document requirements for internal review.
